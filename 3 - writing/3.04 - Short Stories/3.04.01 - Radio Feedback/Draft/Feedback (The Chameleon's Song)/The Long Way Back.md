Tuck walks around, realizing that many more houses have people visiting. This town was a town that people left. It was like every other town that was just like every other town. But people like Oli gave him hope. Maybe with enough room and a little luck, she would make something here. Something that was that place. Her restaurant already did that to some extent, but what she had just told him was a glimpse of something more.

He strolls through the street; considers why he's never really settled down; searches himself for any part that minds and then continues walking. He loves a prairie sky. 

Gets back to the cottage. He's about to get into the truck when he notices the basement light on and goes to investigate.
